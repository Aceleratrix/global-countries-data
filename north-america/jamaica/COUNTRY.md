## Basic Information
- **ISO 3166-1 alpha-2**: JM
- **ISO 3166-1 alpha-3**: JAM
- **Capital**: Kingston
- **Largest City**: Kingston
- **Population (2023 estimate)**: 2,825,544
- **Area**: 10,991 km²
- **Currency**: Jamaican Dollar (JMD, $)
- **Official Language**: English
- **Government Type**: Parliamentary constitutional monarchy
- **Current Monarch**: King Charles III (represented by Governor-General Patrick Allen)
- **Prime Minister**: Andrew Holness

## Geography
Jamaica is an island country in the Caribbean Sea, part of the Greater Antilles. It lies approximately 145 km south of Cuba and 191 km west of Hispaniola. The island is mountainous, with the Blue Mountains range running northeast to southwest, and coastal plains in the north and south.

## Economy
Jamaica's economy is driven by tourism, bauxite/alumina mining, agriculture (sugar, bananas, coffee), and remittances. It is a middle-income country with a developing market economy.

## Culture
Known for reggae music (Bob Marley), Rastafari culture, jerk cuisine, and cricket. The country has a rich Afro-Caribbean heritage.

## Key Facts
- Independence from the United Kingdom: August 6, 1962
- Member of the Commonwealth of Nations, CARICOM, and the United Nations
- Famous for its beaches, Blue Mountain Coffee, and athletic prowess (Usain Bolt)
