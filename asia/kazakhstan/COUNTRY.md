# Kazakhstan 🇰🇿

## Basic Information
- **Official Name**: Republic of Kazakhstan
- **Capital**: Astana
- **Largest City**: Almaty
- **Population**: 19,300,000 (2023 estimate)
- **Area**: 2,724,900 km² (9th largest country globally)
- **GDP**: $0.20 trillion (62nd largest economy globally, 2023 estimate)
- **GDP per Capita**: ~$10,500 (2023 estimate)
- **Currency**: Kazakhstani Tenge (KZT)
- **Official Languages**: Kazakh, Russian
- **Government Type**: Unitary presidential republic
- **Current President**: Kassym-Jomart Tokayev
- **Current Prime Minister**: Olzhas Bektenov
- **Independence Date**: December 16, 1991 (from the Soviet Union)
- **UN Region**: Central Asia

## Key Facts
- Landlocked country bordered by Russia (north, west), China (east), Kyrgyzstan (south), Uzbekistan (south), Turkmenistan (south), and the Caspian Sea (southwest).
- World's largest landlocked country, and 9th largest country overall.
- Holds ~12% of global uranium reserves, making it one of the top uranium producers worldwide.
- Major natural resources: crude oil, natural gas, coal, iron ore, manganese, chromite, lead, zinc, copper, titanium, bauxite, gold, silver.
- Baikonur Cosmodrome, the world's first and largest operational space launch facility, is located in southern Kazakhstan (leased to Russia until 2050).
- Hosted the 2017 Winter Universiade in Almaty, and bid for the 2022 Winter Olympics (lost to Beijing).

## Major Cities
1. Almaty: ~2,000,000 (cultural and financial hub)
2. Astana: ~1,300,000 (capital, former Nur-Sultan 2019-2022)
3. Shymkent: ~1,000,000
4. Aktobe: ~500,000
5. Taraz: ~400,000

## Demographics
- Ethnic groups: Kazakh (70%), Russian (15%), Uzbek (3%), Ukrainian (2%), other (10%)
- Religion: Islam (Sunni, 70%), Christianity (Eastern Orthodox, 25%), other (5%)
- Life expectancy: 73.5 years (2023)
- Population density: 7.1 people per km² (one of the lowest globally)
