# Vietnam

## Overview
Vietnam, officially the Socialist Republic of Vietnam, is a Southeast Asian country located on the eastern edge of the Indochinese Peninsula. It is the world's 53rd largest economy with a GDP of $0.26 trillion (2023 estimates).

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Hanoi (population: ~8.1 million)
- **Largest City**: Ho Chi Minh City (~9.2 million)
- **Population**: 98,168,000 (2023 estimate, 15th globally)
- **Area**: 331,210 km²
- **Currency**: Vietnamese đồng (VND, ₫)
- **Official Language**: Vietnamese
- **Government**: Unitary Marxist-Leninist one-party socialist republic
- **GDP**: $0.26 trillion (53rd globally)
- **GDP per Capita**: ~$2,700 (2023)
- **Neighbors**: China (north), Laos (northwest), Cambodia (southwest)
- **Coastline**: 3,444 km along South China Sea
- **Key Memberships**: ASEAN, United Nations, WTO, APEC

## Economy
Vietnam has transitioned from a planned economy to a socialist-oriented market economy since the Đổi Mới reforms in 1986. Key sectors include:
- Electronics manufacturing (Samsung, Intel facilities)
- Textiles and garments
- Agriculture (world's 2nd largest rice exporter, top coffee exporter)
- Tourism (12.6 million international visitors in 2023)
- Aquaculture (top pangasius and shrimp exporter)

## Demographics
- 54 recognized ethnic groups (Kinh/Viet: 85.3%, Tay: 2%, Thai: 1.9%, others 10.8%)
- Median age: 32.5 years
- Literacy rate: 95.4%
- Urban population: 38.3%

## Geography
- Highest point: Fansipan (3,143 m) in the Hoang Lien Son Mountains
- Major rivers: Red River (north), Mekong River (south)
- Climate: Tropical monsoon, with distinct north-south variations

## UNESCO World Heritage Sites
1. Ha Long Bay (1994)
2. Hoi An Ancient Town (1999)
3. My Son Sanctuary (1999)
4. Phong Nha-Ke Bang National Park (2003)
5. Trang An Landscape Complex (2014)
