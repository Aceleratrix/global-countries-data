# Kyrgyzstan

## Basic Information
- **Capital**: Bishkek
- **Official Languages**: Kyrgyz, Russian
- **Population**: ~6.94 million (2023 estimate)
- **Area**: 199,951 km²
- **Currency**: Kyrgyzstani som (KGS)
- **Calling Code**: +996
- **Internet TLD**: .kg
- **Independence**: August 31, 1991 (from the Soviet Union)
- **Current President**: Sadyr Japarov (since 2021)
- **Prime Minister**: Akylbek Japarov (since 2021)

## Geography
Kyrgyzstan is a landlocked Central Asian country where the Tian Shan mountain range covers over 80% of its territory. It borders Kazakhstan to the north, Uzbekistan to the west, Tajikistan to the south, and China to the east. It is home to Issyk-Kul, the world's second-largest saline lake.

## Economy
The economy relies on agriculture (livestock, cotton), mining (gold, coal, uranium), hydroelectric power, and remittances from citizens working abroad (mostly in Russia). It is a member of the Eurasian Economic Union. GDP (2023): ~$12.3 billion, GDP per capita: ~$1,770.

## Culture
Kyrgyzstan has a rich nomadic heritage, with the Manas Epic (a 500,000-line oral poem) recognized as UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage. Traditional music features the komuz (three-stringed lute). National holidays include Independence Day (August 31) and Nooruz (Persian New Year, March 21).
