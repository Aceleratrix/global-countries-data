# 🇮🇶 Iraq (Republic of Iraq)

## Overview
Iraq, officially the Republic of Iraq, is a country in Western Asia. It is bordered by Turkey to the north, Iran to the east, Kuwait to the southeast, Saudi Arabia to the south, Jordan to the southwest, and Syria to the west. The capital and largest city is Baghdad. Iraq has a population of approximately 43 million people (2023 estimate).

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Baghdad (Population: ~7 million metro area)
- **Population**: 43,000,000 (2023 estimate)
- **Area**: 438,317 km² (169,235 sq mi)
- **GDP**: $0.37 trillion (35th largest economy globally)
- **Currency**: Iraqi Dinar (IQD)
- **Official Languages**: Arabic, Kurdish
- **Government**: Federal parliamentary republic
- **Independence**: October 3, 1932 (from British administration)
- **UN Member**: Yes (Founding member, 1945)

## Economy
Iraq possesses the world's fifth-largest proven oil reserves, with oil exports accounting for over 90% of government revenue. The economy is heavily dependent on the oil sector, which has faced challenges due to conflict and infrastructure damage. Recent efforts focus on economic diversification, reconstruction, and attracting foreign investment.

## Geography
Iraq is dominated by two major rivers, the Tigris and Euphrates, which flow through the country from northwest to southeast. The region between these rivers is historically known as Mesopotamia, one of the cradles of civilization. The country features diverse landscapes including alluvial plain, northern highlands, and western desert.

## History
Home to ancient Mesopotamian civilizations including Sumer, Akkad, Babylon, and Assyria, Iraq has one of the world's oldest cultural histories. Modern Iraq was established after World War I from the Ottoman provinces. The country experienced significant upheaval in the 21st century including the 2003 invasion, sectarian conflict, and the rise and fall of ISIS.
