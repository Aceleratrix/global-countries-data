# Tajikistan (Republic of Tajikistan)
## Basic Information
- Official Name: Republic of Tajikistan
- Capital: Dushanbe
- Area: 143,100 km²
- Population: 10,124,000 (2023 estimate)
- Official Language: Tajik (Persian dialect)
- Inter-ethnic Language: Russian
- Currency: Tajikistani somoni (TJS)
- Calling Code: +992
- ISO Codes: TJ (alpha-2), TJI (alpha-3), 762 (numeric)
- Region: Central Asia

## Geography
- Landlocked, bordered by Afghanistan (south), China (east), Kyrgyzstan (north), Uzbekistan (west)
- Dominated by the Pamir Mountains (Pamir-Alay system), with ~93% of territory mountainous
- Highest Peak: Ismoil Somoni Peak (7,495 m, formerly Communism Peak)
- Major Rivers: Amu Darya (Pyanj), Syr Darya, Zeravshan
- Notable Lakes: Iskanderkul, Karakul
- Climate: Continental, semi-arid in lowlands, polar in high mountains

## Demographics
- Ethnic Groups: Tajik (84.3%), Uzbek (13.8%), Kyrgyz (0.8%), Russian (0.5%), others (0.6%) (2020 census)
- Religion: Islam (98%, Sunni 85%, Shia Ismaili 15%), others (2%)
- Life Expectancy: 71.2 years (2022)
- Literacy Rate: 99.8% (2021)
- Urban Population: 28.5% (2023)

## Economy
- GDP (Nominal): $8.6 billion (2023)
- GDP (PPP): $42.1 billion (2023)
- GDP per Capita (Nominal): $850 (2023)
- Key Sectors: Agriculture (cotton, wheat, fruits), Mining (gold, aluminum), Remittances (30% of GDP from Russia)
- Poverty Rate: 26.3% (2022)
- Unemployment Rate: 11.2% (2023)
- Main Exports: Aluminum, gold, cotton, fruits

## Government
- System: Unitary presidential republic
- President: Emomali Rahmon (since 6 November 1994)
- Legislature: Supreme Assembly (Majlisi Oli), bicameral (National Assembly and Assembly of Representatives)
- Independence: 9 September 1991 (from Soviet Union)
- Key Parties: People's Democratic Party of Tajikistan

## Culture
- Public Holidays: Nowruz (21 March), Independence Day (9 September)
- Traditional Crafts: Carpet weaving, embroidery, ceramics, metalwork
- Music: Shashmaqam (classical Central Asian music)
- Folk Instruments: Dutar, tanbur
- Cuisine Staples: Plov (rice pilaf), samosas, qurut (dried yogurt balls), non (flatbread)

## History
- 9th-10th century: Part of Samanid Empire
- 1860s-1880s: Annexed by Russian Empire
- 1929: Established as Tajik Soviet Socialist Republic
- 1991: Independence from Soviet Union
- 1992-1997: Civil War
- 2000s-present: Post-war reconstruction and growth

## Tourism
- Key Attractions: Pamir Highway, Iskanderkul Lake, Penjikent ancient city, Fann Mountains
- Activities: Mountaineering, trekking, cultural tours
- Annual Tourists: ~120,000 (2023)
- Tourism GDP Contribution: 1.2% (2023)

## Education
- Compulsory Education: 9 years (ages 7-16)
- Literacy Rate: 99.8% (2021)
- Higher Education: Tajik National University, Khujand State University
- Primary Enrollment: 96% (2023)

## Healthcare
- System: Universal public healthcare
- Life Expectancy: 71.2 years (2022)
- Infant Mortality: 25 per 1,000 live births (2022)
- Physicians per 1,000 people: 2.7 (2020)
- Public Health Expenditure: 2.8% of GDP (2022)

## Infrastructure
- Road Network: ~28,000 km (paved: ~18,000 km)
- Railways: ~680 km
- Main Airports: Dushanbe International, Khujand Airport
- Internet Penetration: 35% (2023)
- Mobile Penetration: 98% (2023)
- Electricity Access: 100% (2023)
