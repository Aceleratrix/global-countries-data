# 🇪🇹 Ethiopia

## Country Overview
- **Official Name:** Federal Democratic Republic of Ethiopia
- **Capital:** Addis Ababa
- **Population:** 126,527,060 (2024 estimate)
- **Area:** 1,104,300 km²
- **Currency:** Ethiopian Birr (ETB)
- **Languages:** Amharic (official), Oromo, Tigrinya, Somali, Afar, and 80+ other languages
- **Government:** Federal parliamentary republic
- **Independence:** Ancient civilization; modern state established 1995 (current constitution)
- **GDP:** $0.18 trillion USD (67th largest economy globally)

## Key Facts
- Ethiopia is Africa's second-most populous country after Nigeria
- Origin of coffee (Kaffa region)
- Addis Ababa is the headquarters of the African Union
- Home to ancient Christian kingdoms and rock-hewn churches of Lalibela (UNESCO World Heritage)
- The Blue Nile originates from Lake Tana in Ethiopia
- Hosted the 2024 African Union Summit

## Major Cities
1. **Addis Ababa** - Capital, ~5 million people
2. **Dire Dawa** - ~500,000 people
3. **Mekelle** - ~400,000 people
4. **Gondar** - ~350,000 people
5. **Bahir Dar** - ~330,000 people

## Economy
- Agriculture employs ~70% of population
- Major exports: Coffee, gold, leather products, oilseeds, flowers
- Growing manufacturing sector (textiles, cement)
- Grand Ethiopian Renaissance Dam (GERD) - Africa's largest hydroelectric dam
- Member of African Continental Free Trade Area (AfCFTA)

## Demographics
- **Ethnic Groups:** Oromo (34%), Amhara (27%), Tigray (7%), Somali (6%), Sidama (4%), others
- **Religion:** Ethiopian Orthodox Christianity (43%), Islam (34%), Protestant (18%), traditional beliefs
- **Median Age:** 19.5 years
- **Life Expectancy:** 67 years

## Geography
- Located in Horn of Africa
- Borders: Eritrea, Djibouti, Somalia, Kenya, South Sudan, Sudan
- Great Rift Valley runs through the country
- Diverse topography: highlands, lowlands, deserts
- Simien Mountains National Park (UNESCO World Heritage)

## International Relations
- Member of: African Union, UN, World Bank, IMF, Intergovernmental Authority on Development (IGAD)
- Hosts African Union headquarters
- Strategic partner of China, US, EU
- Active in regional peacekeeping and diplomacy

## Recent Developments
- 2018: Peace agreement with Eritrea (ended 20-year conflict)
- 2020-2022: Tigray conflict
- 2023: Pretoria Peace Agreement
- Ongoing economic reforms and privatization
- 2024: Continued focus on infrastructure development and regional integration

*Data sourced from World Bank, UN, IMF, and Ethiopian government sources (2024)*
