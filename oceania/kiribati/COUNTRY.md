# Kiribati 🇰🇮

## Overview
Kiribati (official: Republic of Kiribati) is an Oceanian island nation in Micronesia, consisting of 33 atolls and reef islands spanning the equator in the central Pacific Ocean. It has no land borders and is highly vulnerable to sea-level rise.

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Tarawa
- **Population**: ~131,000 (2023 estimate)
- **Area**: 811 km²
- **GDP**: $0.05 trillion (171st largest economy globally)
- **Currency**: Australian dollar (AUD), Kiribati dollar (KID)
- **Languages**: English (official), Gilbertese (national)
- **Region**: Oceania
- **Subregion**: Micronesia
- **Independence**: July 12, 1979 (from United Kingdom)
- **Government**: Unitary parliamentary republic under constitutional monarchy

## Economy
Primary sectors: Fishing, tourism, copra production. GDP per capita ~$3,800. Remittances from overseas workers are a key income source.

## Geography
Low-lying coral atolls with maximum elevation of 81m (Banaba Island). Coastline length: 1,143 km. Home to the Phoenix Islands Protected Area (UNESCO World Heritage Site).

## Demographics
Ethnic groups: 98.9% Gilbertese. Religions: 96% Christian. Literacy rate: 95%. Life expectancy: 67 years.
