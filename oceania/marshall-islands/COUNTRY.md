# Marshall Islands (Republic of the Marshall Islands)
## Overview
The Marshall Islands is a sovereign island country in Oceania, located in the central Pacific Ocean. It consists of 29 atolls and 5 isolated islands, with a total land area of approximately 181 km².

## Key Facts
- **Capital**: Majuro
- **Largest City**: Majuro
- **Population (2023)**: ~42,418
- **Official Languages**: Marshallese, English
- **Currency**: United States Dollar (USD)
- **Calling Code**: +692
- **ISO 3166 Code**: MH (alpha-2), MHL (alpha-3), 584 (numeric)
- **Government**: Unitary parliamentary republic in free association with the United States
- **Current President**: Hilda Heine (re-elected January 2024)
- **National Anthem**: "Forever Marshall Islands"
- **Flag**: Blue field with two diagonal stripes (orange and white) and a white star in the upper hoist corner

## Geography
Situated near the Equator, the Marshall Islands lies between Hawaii and Australia. The atolls are coral reefs encircling lagoons, with the two major island chains being Ratak (Sunrise) and Ralik (Sunset).

## Economy
The economy is primarily driven by the service sector, subsistence agriculture, fishing, and financial assistance from the United States under the Compact of Free Association. Key exports include copra, fish, and seaweed.

## History
Formerly part of the Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands administered by the United States, the Marshall Islands gained independence in 1986. It was the site of significant nuclear testing by the US between 1946 and 1958, including the Bravo test in 1954.
